About Khasta

My name is Huda and I’m the lead designer and baker behind Khasta. Khasta means fresh or crispy, and is a word of Persian origin used in lingo across the Indian Subcontinent when referring to fresh delicacies and street food.


At Khasta, I focus on the bridge between culture and food by using my personal experiences and identity as a launching pad for my creations. I connect my unique background with a passion for baking to create a product that goes much deeper than the simple ingredients I use. Rather, the desserts and sweets I produce are a stepping stone for individuals to better understand themselves, their heritage, and the ever changing world around them.

Acting as a comfort food in an increasingly uncomfortable world, Khasta products act as a constant reminder to find happiness in all those things that make YOU unique. I hope that those who enjoy Khasta products also get the opportunity to enjoy these products as a reminder that your background, your language, your culture, and your food, are all things that can be celebrated.


I was born and raised in Michigan where I first ventured into my passion for baking at an early age. What first began as weekends helping my Nani (Maternal Grandmother) roll and spread her traditional Meethi Roti (Sweet Dal Bread), quickly grew into an array of Desi and American desserts for friends and family.


I refined my culinary skills by completing baking & pastry coursework under the tutelage of Pastry Specialist Chef Keegan Rodgers at the Washtenaw Community College. Thereafter, I moved onwards to Wayne State University where I completed degrees in Nutrition & Food Science as well as Anthropology, further cementing myself at the intersection of food and it’s cross cultural relationship.


The kitchen is my workshop and my ingredients are my tools. Every blank cookie represents an empty canvas where I can pour out my ideas. Whether it’s my experiences wandering the bazaars of Karachi, my childhood summers in Saudi Arabia, my native languages of Urdu and Gujarati, the crescent of Eid, or the dome of the Blue Mosque; my South Asian-American and Islamic background fuels my creativity as I utilize personal themes and motifs to create fusion food. I equally apply this level of passion when working with my clients, understanding my customers unique background as an inspiration to create a truly meaningful and personable product.


I have provided Khasta products for an array of events and customers; from large gatherings like weddings and mehndis, intimate gatherings like nikkahs and bridal showers, children events like aqiqas and birthdays, as well as live demonstrations at events such as the Halal Food Festival. I have competed in local and regional culinary competitions like the Muslim Interscholastic Tournament (MIST), where my work spoke for itself, earning 1st place honors for 4 years straight. Khasta products are now available online and have been delivered to customers across the United States. After many fortunate years in the Metro Detroit area, Khasta has now relocated to the Greater Chicagoland Area where I am excited to work with new communities and people.


Khasta products are not simply foods to be consumed. It’s food for thought and the soul. It’s both the perfect gift and personal splurge. It can be enjoyed with friends or alone. It’s both a conversation starter and a source of reflection. It’s a gateway to learning more about yourself and the world around you. I hope that you can enjoy a fresh product soon so that you too can also become Khasta.
